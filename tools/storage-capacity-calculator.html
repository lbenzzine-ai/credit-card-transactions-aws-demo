Before you present this footprint or cost number

  • 1
    Cold tiers trade cost for retrieval time and fee. S3 Glacier Deep Archive is roughly 23x cheaper than S3 Standard, but retrieval takes 12-48 hours and incurs its own per-GB fee. It's for compliance archives you'll rarely if ever read back — not for anything a live query needs.
  • 2
    Full-projection GSIs are the most overlooked storage cost. ProjectionType=ALL duplicates the entire item per index. If a query only needs a few attributes, KEYS_ONLY or INCLUDE projections can avoid doubling storage for every additional index.
  • 3
    Retention minimums are usually set by compliance, not engineering. Shrinking a retention period to save storage cost is a legal/compliance decision, not a technical one — confirm the actual regulatory requirement before treating retention as a tunable.
  • 4
    Growth compounds more than intuition suggests over long retention windows. A modest 15%/year growth rate roughly triples total volume over a 7-year retention period — small assumptions early in the input meaningfully change the final number.
  • 5
    Cold storage classes have minimum storage durations and object-count overhead. S3 Glacier Deep Archive has a 180-day minimum (early deletion is billed as if it stayed the full period), and each archived object carries roughly 40KB of metadata overhead — this penalizes archiving huge numbers of very small objects.
  • 6
    This covers storage only, not request/throughput costs. DynamoDB read/write request pricing, S3 PUT/GET/LIST request fees, and data transfer costs are separate line items not modeled here — a full cost picture needs those too.
  • 7
    Unit prices are illustrative, not live. Figures shown are approximate us-east-1 rates at a point in time — always check current AWS pricing before using this for a real budget commitment.

Please note

A common, defensible pattern is tiered storage rather than one tier for everything: keep recent, frequently queried data in DynamoDB for millisecond access; move data past a defined age into S3 Standard once it's rarely queried directly; and transition anything held purely for compliance into S3 Glacier Deep Archive once it's essentially never going to be read. The cost difference between tiers is large enough that this lifecycle strategy is usually worth the added complexity for any dataset retained more than a year or two.
Total items = Σ over each year of (daily records × 365 × (1 + growth)^year). Storage = total items × item size × (1 + full-projection GSIs) × replication factor.
